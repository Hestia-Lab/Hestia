## Landing hero

> ### The agent's shielded memory.
> Positions, strategy, and counterparties live as a proof, not a public record. Your agent proves what it must and reveals nothing else.
>
> **Hestia hid the funds. Lethe hides the mind.**

**Three blocks:**

**Remember privately**
An agent's memory is a commitment, not a public variable. It only moves under proven, well-formed rules. No one reads it without the agent's key.

**Prove without showing**
Prove a property of hidden state ("balance over X", "within policy", "we agreed on this price") in zero knowledge. The fact crosses. The memory stays.

**Negotiate with hidden hands**
Two agents settle a deal against each other's hidden state, each proving its half. Neither reads the other's mind. The proof is the trust.

## Honesty guardrails (do not violate)

- Lethe is concept + early build. Do NOT label it "live", "verified", or "audited". Hestia is the part that's test-verified; Lethe inherits that core but its own channel/e2e is the remaining build.
- "shield the links" not "hide the source/origin" (avoid money-laundering framing; Hestia proves clean origin, it does not hide it).
- The trusted setup is dev/single-contributor: never imply production security before the ceremony.
- Tease only what will actually follow soon; a teaser with no follow-through reads as vaporware.
